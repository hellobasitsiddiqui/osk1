OpenSkeleton Help

Signing in, your profile, and where to get support.

What is OpenSkeleton?

OpenSkeleton is a full-stack application skeleton — the shared foundation a team starts from when building a new product. It bundles the essentials (a backend API, a web front-end, native mobile shells, authentication, and a cloud deploy path) so a new app can be up and running without rebuilding the basics each time.

This site is that web front-end. Once you sign in you get your own account and profile; the pages here also expose the service status and the operations panel.

Signing in

There are three intended ways to sign in. Pick whichever suits you — they all sign you in to the same account.

Email code (the default)

Enter your email address and we send you a one-time sign-in code. Type that code back into the app to sign in — there is no password to remember. This is the method the app offers first.

Text message (SMS)

Prefer a code by text instead? On the sign-in screen choose Try another way and select the phone / SMS option. We text a one-time code to your mobile number; enter it to sign in.

Email and password

If you set up a password on your account, you can sign in the classic way with your email address and that password.

Editing your profile

Once signed in, open your profile from the account menu to review and update your details — your display name, contact email, and the other preferences held against your account.

Make your changes and save them; they take effect straight away. If a field can't be changed from here (for example an email tied to how you sign in), the app will tell you and point you to the right place.

Getting support

Stuck, or something not working as described here? We're happy to help.

Email us at support@openskeleton.dev and include what you were doing and what you expected to happen.

For live service health, check the status page first — it will tell you if there is a known outage.
